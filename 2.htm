PlayStation 5: a name as evidence

Sony took the floor in early October 2019 to take stock of its new generation console and confirm its name. The PS5 will be called PlayStation 5. A name that will not surprise anyone, since it will be the fifth home console of the manufacturer. Twenty-six years after the first PlayStation, the PS5 will have to confirm the good health of the Japanese giant who dominated the eighth generation of consoles. Sony has sold more than 100 million PlayStation systems worldwide, while rival Microsoft is struggling to reach the 50 million Xbox mark

In June 2020, the consoler however confirmed a notable element: his PS5 will not be the only device available to players. They will have the choice between a standard model, with a disc player (Blu-ray Ultra HD), and a console that will not. This Digital Edition, slightly thinner and probably a little cheaper, will therefore require to download all the games on its storage space
